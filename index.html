Basketball Game Simulation

Sports Analytics final project

My Sports analytics class was focused on analyzing, predicting, and ranking team and player performance. Using publicly available datasets we made our own Simple Rating Systems, simulated NCAA tournaments, and examined interesting metrics of our choice. We had creative liberty on what we wanted to do for our final project, so I wanted to make my own NCAA basketball simulator. Given two teams season data (at a play by play level), key metrics would be examined to come up with possession result probabilities which would be used to simulate games on a possession by possession basis.
This project strengthened my analytical and critical thinking skills by identifying key metrics and translating them into probabilities that drove the simulation. Extensive amounts of interpreting csv files was needed which improved my data analytics skills. This project also showcased my ability to work independently, manage complex tasks, and think creatively to transform raw data into actionable insights.
